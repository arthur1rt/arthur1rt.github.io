Drifters Don't Brake (2020)

Drifters Don't Brake provides a very satisfying drifting experience.
D.D.B. differs from other games of the genre by not allowing the player to control whether the car is accelerating or not. In D.D.B. your car is always accelerating! The only way of reducing the car's speed is by drifting.

Play the beta on Itch.io and wishlist it on Steam!

Drifters Don't Brake was originally made for the GMTK2020 gamejam. The jam's theme was Out of Control and I decided to create a game where you can't brake, only drift.
After the jam, I felt potential in the game and decided to keep working on it.

The game is now on Steam, and has a free beta version for download on Itch.io.

Chat Clan Wars (2019)

Hello twitch streamer! This game was made for you to interact with your chat.
Chat Clan Wars is an RTS game where each of your viewers can control his/her own player. Using commands on the Twitch chat like !join, !attack, or !build, they will have to work as a team to win the match.

Chat Clan Wars is an online game. However, it has to be played using the Twitch Chat. The game's purpose is to be streamed by someone and played by his/hers viewers (on twitch.tv).

Square x Square (2017)

Square x Square is a local PvP game (up to 4 players) where your objective is to defeat your opponents. You may choose a helmet, a weapon and a perk to battle against your friends (or enemies). On the stage, a chest is randomly generated. The player who collects the chest will trigger a global random event.

Square x Square was developed while I was going to College on 2016. It was an old stopped project. Then I showed it to my friend and he told me:

Then I finished it!

Darrow (2017)

Darrow is a project made in GameMaker: Studio to study Client-Server communication.
The server creates rooms to support 2 players. When both players join a room, the game starts. When a room has already 2 players and another player log in, the server automatically creates another room to accommodate the third player.

This was my first experience coding a networking game. Definitely a good challenge for that time, and a lot of fun!

Jack In The Maze (2017)

Jack In The Maze is a survivor-like game where you have to clear all the stage to get to a new one. Also, you can get upgrades on your abilities and increase your stats. The objective of the game is to survive the longest possible.
Collect green boxes to get ammo, and the white boxes to get life.

I developed Jack in the maze to study random generated map and real-time minimap. The game ended up pretty fun and challenging!

Chest Cover (2016)

Chest cover is an endless game which will store your best scores on each of the stages you play. You will have to protect the chest from the enemies to get more points. However, this task won’t be so easy since you have to walk around to get yourself more ammo. Also, enemies get stronger with time

I developed Chest Cover on about 2 weeks. I did it to learn a bit more about data saving/loading.

[GameJam] MonSTEROID (2015)

You've been bullied on school for your whole life. Then, you decided to get stronger to beat all those bullies up! The day has finally came!
Use your five skills to get to the bullies and beat them up. Take care, they have many allies!

MonSTEROID is a game developed for the LudumDare#33 GameJam.
The theme was “You are the Monster” and we had 48h to develop a game.
We decided to play with the theme and tell a story of a boy who becomes a muscle monster using steroids to beat up the bullies from his school.
This was my very first finished game. Although it might look weird at first, I’m sure it is very challenging and fun.
Also, we’ve got many good reviews on LudumDare’s website.
